Career Express
Having trouble finding a job? The Career Express Lab is located at the Prosperity Center to help you gain access to job leads and professional development opportunities. Career Express allows you to receive job search coaching on a walk-in basis without having to schedule an appointment in advance. Within Career Express, we can discuss job search strategies, critique your résumé and cover letter, help you practice your interview skills, answer questions about our job openings, and much more! Career Express is available to anyone looking for a job in Tallahassee. When you arrive at the Prosperity Center, our receptionist will have you sign-in for Career Express at the front desk. Then, you can access our Career Express lab.

Hours in the Career Express Lab:

Monday - Thursday, 8:00a.m - 4:00p.m.

Talk Shop with Career Volunteers in the Prosperity Center training room allows you an opportunity to chat with Career Volunteers and other job searchers on Wednesdays from 3 - 4 p.m.

We also hold job clubs regularly, that are focused on certain groups. If you have been having difficulty finding a job because of criminal convictions, join our R.E.A.C.H. (Re-Entry Employment Access in Communities with Helpers) which provides help and support for ex-offenders as they re-enter society and the workforce and helps to coordinate resources for individuals whose criminal record creates barriers to self-sufficiency. To enroll, contact Oceo Harris by email or by calling (850) 576-7145.

The Prosperity Center offers Youth Career Services, for youth between the ages of 12 and 17. We provide workshops including:

  • Finding Your Spark
  • Self-Employment for Youth
  • Career Exploration activities

Please see our schedule for dates or email Dr. Claudette Harrell.

For those youth looking for a summer or year-round job, contact our Career Volunteer, Lynette, via email or call (850) 576-7145.

Fitness
Exercise is an activity many people enjoy to strengthen bodies and calm minds. Too often, people with lower incomes and people with disabilities are left out of this part of a prosperous life. Numerous studies show that low-income children and adults are far more likely to be overweight than those of greater means. And the statistical distribution fits a nice, neat curve – as income falls, the rate of obesity rises. In today’s world, the cheapest calories come from junk foods – whether those foods are found at the grocery store, the gas station, or in the fast food restaurant, conveniently located just down the street.

Fitness and wellness activities are also beneficial for those living with catastrophic illness, injury and secondary health conditions related to long-term disabilities. Until now, facilities have been designed for short-term rehabilitation, and even those often become off-limits after insurance coverage expires.

The Prosperity Center Fitness Center is completely focused on empowering and educating its members to obtain the best physical exercise possible by providing the latest in fully accessible equipment. We want to encourage and give information about wellness to people in Tallahassee, regardless of income or disability. The fee for membership is $10 monthly, with no contract. When you first come in, we will ask you to complete a fitness center member application and health questionnaire. Once that is completed, you can schedule an appointment with our Fitness Representative who will work with you to discuss your fitness and wellness goals, and help you to develop a workout plan. Request a meeting by email with our Fitness Representative now, or call (850) 576-7145

We also offer Yoga, Zumba, Cardio and Aerobic Classes, free to the public each week. Please see our calendar and join us for a good workout, in an inclusive, fun environment.

Disabilities

The Prosperity Center believes that each person with a disability should be empowered to function at their highest achievable level of independence and that each person, regardless of age, race, religion, diagnosis, income or gender can both give and gain from these community services.  Our strategy is to combine a wide range of opportunities, individual advocacy and the provision of direct services.  The Prosperity Center believes in the principles of customer choice, mutual respect and equal opportunity for all people in Tallahassee.

Our facility is accessible and on the T and F route of Tallahassee's bus line. We provide equipment accessible to people with varying disabilities and provide the level guidance, structure and assistance you want. You are our customer and we are grateful you are here!

Financial Strengthening

Sometimes it takes a little help to get on solid financial footing. Our financial mentors are here to help you manage your debt and expenses to better your financial situation. The Prosperity Center focuses on breaking down barriers that keep individuals from being successful in reaching their goal of financial independence. This program is designed to be a hand up and not just a hand out.

The Prosperity Center offers Tallahassee a one-stop source of financial stability services, including: Free Income Tax Preparation, Credit Counseling & Financial Education, Money Management & Budget Development, Educational & Career Counseling, First-time Homebuyer Education, Micro-Enterprise Education, Asset Building through savings and banking basics, Foreclosure Mitigation (intake); and Benefits Screening  & Enrollment (access to food stamps, Kidcare, Medicare,  Medicaid, etc.).

We also have monthly financial challenges with awards for winners to those enrolled into our Money Clubs. For more information, email Marisa Thomas.

Music

The Prosperity Center of Tallahassee offers a state of the art Music Hall and music practice rooms in order to bring a higher quality of music education to people of all levels. Our vision is to share our love of music with people of all ages, all income levels and all abilities. We offer anyone who wants to enrich their life with music the opportunity to learn at his or her own pace, with renowned experts. These classes are provided FREE of charge to the public and is supported through Tallahassee’s citizens’ donations to Goodwill Industries.

Beginners, whether youth or adult, may learn any of a number of Brass, Wind and Percussion instruments, which are provided by the Prosperity Center. We offer our students the flexibility to master learning music and their instrument at their own pace with individual instruction. Intermediate players can join with the practicing intermediate band to further their skills in a group setting and music masters can participate in our publicly performing band, The Spirit of Goodwill Community Band.

Our Music Center is headed by the renowned, Dr. Julian White is a retired distinguished professor of Music and the former conductor of the world famous Marching 100. He has conducted bands for the Grammys, for international music festivals and the President of the United States. He is a dedicated teacher and invites you to join him and other students in experiencing lifelong inspiration and joy through music. 

To sign up for the 2016 FREE Summer Band Camp, with Dr. Julian White, please view the Information Letter and complete the 2016 Summer Band Camp Application.

To sign up for FREE lessons, please complete the Music Lesson Information Form.
To volunteer to give musical instruction, please complete the Volunteer Form.

Or call us at (850) 576-7145, ext. 170

Training

Goodwill Industries has been training people for careers since 1902! We know what employers want. Visit Career Campus at www.gwcareercampus.com to take classes in Hospitality, Office Administration, Retail, Finance, Entrepreneurship, and more. Some courses require hands-on training at a Career Campus Lab, in addition to the web content. All our courses are FREE other than the cost of optional workbooks and optional certification exams.

Our two main Career Campuses are in Panama City and Tallahassee, with satellite labs in Chipley, Valparaiso, Marianna, DeFuniak Springs, and Quincy. We also have a lab in Thomasville, Georgia. Go to www.gwcareercampus.com to find the Career Campus lab nearest you!

Youth

Our Youth Mentoring program, GoodGuides, works to assist youth in high-risk situations to make better life choices. The unemployment rate for youth ages 16 to 19 is currently at nearly 21 percent-three times the national average for the full working population – and even higher here in Tallahassee. The GoodGuides program helps to keep these youth on the right path, despite the employment challenges they face. The result is stronger families and better communities.

At this critical moment, with high youth unemployment discouraging many teens, young people need positive role models who show them that they can indeed succeed. Made possible through a grant from the U.S. Department of Justice, the GoodGuides Mentoring Program offers training and mentor support, advocacy and teaching roles for mentors, expanded parent engagement and more structured activities for participating youth.

The GoodGuides program serves any youth, between the ages of 12 and 17, who want to learn about careers and prepare for success. It helps them build career plans and skills and prepare for school completion, post-secondary training and productive work. To refer a youth to the program, call or email Darrell Feagin at (850) 576-7145  ext 175.

If you would like to become a Mentor, please see our Volunteer Descriptions.
